Origin Story...

I began my journey in the year 2004 after graduating from Delaware Skills Center. I always had interest in fixing and experimenting with things growing up. Learning electrical there fed that natural curiosity I had. Upon completion a friend of mine wanted me to check out low-voltage work. I took him up on that, and now 20+ years later, I've made a sucessful career out of it. Let me fill in the blanks for you...

Learning, Growing...

I started as a "grunt", not really understanding how stuff worked. When my foreman said something like:"Put the green wire under the 3rd terminal", I got pretty good at simply blindly following direction. After about 3 years of this, that natural curiosity started to show itself once again. I started asking not just "what...?" but also "why...?" and especially how...?". While this constant questioning may have annoyed many, thankfully I was surrounded by people that appreciated the fact I wasn't comfortable with just doing what I was told.

Trusting the Process...

After getting many of my questions answered, I started to really understand the theories behind those directions I followed blindly in the past. Now, I would think to myself :"The green wire goes under the 3rd terminal, sure. But it goes there because thats where the controller recieves data from a device in the field". I was really coming into my own, then I met two individuals who completely shredded everything I thought I knew: Andy Capoferri and "Jimmy the K" aka Jim Kompania. They made me question the questions and answers I had amassed to that point, but deep inside, I loved the challenge despite the frustration I usually showed. I'll always be indebted to those guys for my carreer trajectory from that point on.

Currently...

Fast forward 20+ years from Jimmy the K and Andy, and I've done pretty much everything I can do in my industry. Cue that pesky curiosity again. Now it has led me to LaunchCode, trying to question yet again, everything I've learned to this point in my career. I'm certain I'll get the answers I need in the process and reach heights unknown in the next 20 years.
